# Old Sebilis

Before the Combine Spire fell, before the Iksar were broken, there was Sebilis — the crown of the Sebilisian Empire. A city of jade and obsidian built on the backs of every enslaved race the Iksar could reach. Now it rots beneath the jungle floor, half-submerged, inhabited only by the undead remnants of its former glory and the froglok servants who never found their way out.

## The Fall

The Iksar Empire did not simply collapse. It was unmade — through plague, through the wrath of the dragonkind, through internal betrayal, through the slow weight of every wrong it committed pressing down until the stone cracked. Sebilis sank into the swamp as though the earth itself wanted the city to disappear.

## Trakanon

The dragon **Trakanon** now occupies what remains of the Sebilisian throne room. He is one of the Sleeper's Tomb guardians, an elder dragon driven to madness by the poison in his own veins. His breath does not burn — it *rots*, corrupting flesh and spirit alike. He is intelligent, ancient, and furious at everything that still lives.

Those who seek him out often do not return. Those who do are changed.

## What Remains

- The frogloks who were once slaves here have never left. They wander the ruins in something between servitude and haunting.
- The necromancers of the Iksar still whisper from beyond death, their spirits bound to the architecture they built.
- Venril Sathir, greatest of the Iksar warlords, persists somewhere in these ruins — unwilling to be truly dead.

## Lore Threads

- The throne room contains carvings that predate the Iksar Empire — carved by something that came before and was buried to make room.
- The frogloks have a word for what Sebilis has become. It does not translate to Common, but it sounds like grief.
