# The Ruins of Kunark

Kunark is not a continent so much as a wound.

The Iksar built their empire here and called it glory. They built it on the bodies of every race they could reach and called the architecture of suffering *civilization*. And then the empire fell — not cleanly, not quickly, but through plague and dragon-fire and treachery and the slow collapse of a system that had nothing holding it together except force.

What remains is jungle reclaiming stonework, undead servants still performing their duties for masters who have been dust for centuries, and a dragon named Trakanon sitting on a throne of bones wondering when something worth destroying will finally arrive.

## The Continent

Kunark is vast and treacherous. Its jungles hide ruins that hide older ruins that hide things that do not have names in any living language. The Iksar who survived the fall of their empire rebuilt in smaller forms — scattered city-states, each convinced it is the rightful heir to something that has already proven it cannot hold.

### Key Regions

- **The Overthere** — a plateau of sarnak warlords and lizardmen, endlessly at war
- **Firiona Vie** — the elven outpost, the toehold of the western world in a hostile continent
- **The Field of Bone** — open plains scattered with the literal bones of things that died here
- **Sebilis** — what the capital became when the empire finished dying

## What Brought Me Here

The continent calls to necromancers the way rot calls to beetles. The Iksar understood death as a resource. Their libraries contain things that have not been read by living eyes for three hundred years. The ruins contain things that should not be disturbed — which is, for certain purposes, exactly the point.

## The Timeline

- **Age of Scale** — dragons rule Kunark; no Iksar yet exist
- **Age of Blood** — Iksar emerge; the Sebilisian Empire rises through conquest
- **Age of War** — the fall; plague, dragon-wrath, internal collapse; the empire unmade
- **Present** — ruins, scattered survivors, opportunists, and whatever comes next
